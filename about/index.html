About This Platform

This platform is designed to monitor and document developments that shape interethnic relations in Kosovo, with the aim of supporting informed analysis, early warning, and confidence-building. The platform captures three core categories of developments:

  • Interethnic Incidents: Acts or events—physical, verbal, symbolic, or institutional—that reflect tensions or conflict between communities on the basis of ethnic identity. Examples include hate speech, ethnically motivated violence or vandalism, and discriminatory practices or decisions.
  • Positive Developments: Actions, initiatives, or events that foster peacebuilding and interethnic understanding, cooperation, or reconciliation. This can include joint community projects, inclusive policy decisions, public statements promoting coexistence, cross-community cultural or educational exchanges, and community leader efforts.
  • Institutional Responses: Official actions taken by local or central institutions in response to incidents or developments affecting interethnic relations. These may include public condemnations, disciplinary or legal action, policy interventions, or outreach efforts aimed at de-escalation or reconciliation.

Source Base and Verification

The platform is primarily based on secondary sources, including local and national media outlets, verified NGO reports, and publicly available institutional communications. All entries undergo basic verification procedures to assess credibility and minimize misinformation. However, as the platform relies on publicly accessible reporting and is subject to practical limitations, it does not claim to be exhaustive. We make our best efforts to ensure regular updates and balanced coverage, but some developments may go unreported or unverified. Therefore, the content should not be interpreted as a definitive or authoritative record of all interethnic dynamics in Kosovo.
