About This Platform

The Risk Monitor Platform on Interethnic Relations is an interactive tool developed to track and visualize key developments—both risks and positive examples—that impact interethnic relations in Kosovo. Hosted within our existing website, this platform serves as a real-time monitoring system that supports early warning efforts, fosters transparency, and informs decision-making for peacebuilding and social cohesion. At its core is a dynamic map of Kosovo that highlights incidents, public statements, and events with potential interethnic implications. Users can click on specific municipalities to access verified news reports tied to that location. Each entry includes: Date and location Brief event summary Photograph (where available) Bilingual content (Albanian and Serbian) In addition to documenting risks—such as violence, political tensions, or inflammatory rhetoric—the platform is also designed to highlight positive examples of interethnic cooperation, such as joint initiatives, community reconciliation efforts, and inclusive governance actions. By capturing both challenges and progress, the platform offers a balanced, evidence-based perspective on the state of interethnic relations in Kosovo. All entries are curated by our team, which regularly monitors credible sources to ensure accuracy and relevance. The reports are also compiled into a centralized, filterable table to support deeper analysis and policy response. The platform is intended for use by donors, policymakers, civil society actors, researchers, and the broader public who seek to better understand interethnic dynamics in Kosovo and contribute to sustainable peace and coexistence.

This site is part of a project supported by NED (National Endowment for Democracy), titled 'Increasing Government Transparency and Accountability in Interethnic Dialogue,' and implemented by KCSS. The authors wrote the specific chapters within their own capacities. As such, the views presented in this site do not necessarily reflect the views of KCSS or NED.
